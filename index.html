Research Profile

Every production system I have built has had the same hidden layer underneath it: a set of rules that determine what is allowed, what is not, and what happens at the boundary. In government, that meant eligibility logic for citizens navigating residential construction approvals in Abu Dhabi. In healthcare, it meant privacy and verification constraints on clinical data flowing across decentralised networks. In fintech, it meant KYC conditions and payment compliance gates that could not be wrong.

Over seven years of encoding these rules into software, I kept encountering the same gap — the rules existed in legislation, but the verification of whether a system actually followed them was manual, fragmented, or absent entirely. That gap is the research problem I intend to pursue.

My focus is on how regulatory compliance obligations can be formally modelled as machine-checkable logic and automatically verified against real event data — starting with Australia's livestock movement traceability system, where the stakes of non-compliance are measurable, the regulations are well-defined, and the digital infrastructure is mid-transition.

Automated Compliance Verification for Interstate Livestock Movements into NSW: A Rule-Based Conformance Engine for the NLIS eID Transition (2025–2027)

Question How can deterministic interstate livestock movement compliance obligations — including cross-jurisdictional identification requirements under NSW Clause 18(1)(b) and related NLIS law recognition under Clause 17(1)(b) — be formally encoded as machine-evaluable rules and automatically verified against standardised traceability event data during the NLIS eID transition (2025–2027)?
Methodology Design Science Research — artifact development (compliance rule model + verification module) and evaluation against synthetic event datasets for detection accuracy, throughput/latency at scale, and outbreak trace-back utility.
